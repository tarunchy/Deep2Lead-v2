1 Fine-Tuning Methodology

QLoRA via Unsloth — Approach Overview

We used Unsloth's FastModel + PEFT QLoRA to fine-tune the 4-bit quantized Gemma4-E2B model. Rather than modifying the full model (~2B parameters), we inserted low-rank adapter matrices into 7 attention and feed-forward layers (~1% of parameters are trainable). This lets us teach the model drug-discovery vocabulary and SMILES grammar without catastrophic forgetting of general language ability.

7 Analysis — What We Learned

✅ Clear Wins (Fine-tuned)

  • SMILES validity +22.5pp — from 65% to 87.5%. The most important drug discovery metric. Model learned proper SMILES ring closure, aromatic notation, valence rules.
  • QED improvement — +0.009 aggregate, +0.115 on HIV Protease. More drug-like molecules.
  • Heterocycle handling — BRAF target: 25% → 100% validity. Fine-tuning taught sulfonamide + thiazole SMILES grammar correctly.
  • Halogen diversity — Mpro target: fine-tuned generates F/Cl/Br variants, production sticks to fluorine only.

⚠️ Issues to Fix (v2)

  • Low uniqueness (12.5–25%) — Model often repeats the same SMILES. Cause: MOSES training data reinforced identical outputs. Fix: repetition penalty, nucleus sampling tuning.
  • High Tanimoto to seed — Fine-tuned stays too close to the seed (0.921 avg). Needs more diverse training examples exploring scaffold space.
  • Latency 2.1× slower — FastAPI inference vs optimised vLLM. Fixable by switching to vLLM serving.
  • Only 40K training samples — SMolInstruct (3M) would significantly improve quality. Fix the Parquet downloader for v2.

Diversity level: 0.50 (0=minimal change, 1=large structural variation)

Rules:
- Output ONLY valid SMILES strings, one per line, no numbering or explanation
- Each molecule must differ from the seed (substitute atoms, add/remove groups)
- Follow Lipinski's Rule of Five: MW<500, LogP<5, HBD<=5, HBA<=10
- Do not repeat molecules
